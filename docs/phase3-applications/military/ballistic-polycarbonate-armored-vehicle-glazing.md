# Ballistic Polycarbonate Glazing Supply for Armored Vehicle Manufacturers

Ballistic glazing is the most demanding polycarbonate application. Every panel must stop a specific projectile at a specific velocity — guaranteed.

## Ballistic Protection Levels

| Level | Threat | Velocity | PC Laminate Thickness |
|-------|--------|---------|----------------------|
| NIJ II | 9mm FMJ | 398 m/s | 15-18mm |
| NIJ IIIA | .44 Magnum | 436 m/s | 20-25mm |
| NIJ III | 7.62mm NATO | 847 m/s | Glass-clad PC, 40-60mm |
| STANAG 4569 L1 | 5.56mm | 900 m/s | 50-70mm glass/PC laminate |
| STANAG 4569 L2 | 7.62mm API | 695 m/s | Glass/PC/glass composite |

## Why PC in Ballistic Laminates

Pure glass ballistic glazing is heavy (50 kg/m² for NIJ III) and spalls (glass fragments on interior from non-penetrating impacts). PC as the interior layer catches spall and provides the final energy absorption. The standard construction: glass strike face → PVB interlayer → PC backing → polyurethane inner layer.

## Supplier Requirements

- NIJ or STANAG certified test reports from accredited lab (not self-certified)
- Lot-level ballistic testing (statistical sampling, not 100%)
- 5-year delamination warranty
- ITAR compliance for export-controlled specifications
- ISO 9001 minimum; IATF 16949 preferred

## Q: What thickness for cash-in-transit vehicle windows?

NIJ IIIA (20-25mm PC laminate) is standard for CIT vehicles. Weight is the limiting factor — thicker = heavier, affecting vehicle payload and fuel economy.
