# Transparent Armor Polycarbonate — NIJ and STANAG Procurement Guide

Procuring ballistic polycarbonate for government or institutional use requires navigating certification, testing, and compliance requirements.

## Certification Standards

| Standard | Jurisdiction | Key Requirement |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| NIJ 0108.01 | USA | Ballistic resistant protective materials |
| STANAG 4569 | NATO | Protection levels for logistic/light armored vehicles |
| VPAM APR 2006 | Germany | Attack-resistant glazing |
| GOST R 51136 | Russia | Bulletproof glass |
| UL 752 | International | Bullet-resisting equipment |

## Procurement Documentation

Every ballistic PC panel must be accompanied by: NIJ/STANAG test report from accredited lab (UL, HP White, Beschussamt), lot number traceable to specific panels, certificate of conformance, and installation certification (ballistic performance depends on correct frame installation — a certified panel in an uncertified frame is not ballistic).

## Supply Chain Security

Government procurement requires: ITAR/EAR compliance for export-controlled items, supplier facility security clearance (for classified specs), domestic manufacturing preference (Berry Amendment for US DoD), and supply chain traceability to raw material source.
