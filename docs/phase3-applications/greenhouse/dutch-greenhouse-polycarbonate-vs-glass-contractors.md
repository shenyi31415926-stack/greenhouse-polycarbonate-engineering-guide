# Dutch-Style Commercial Greenhouse — Polycarbonate vs Glass for Contractors

Dutch greenhouse contractors build the world's highest-performance growing environments. The PC vs glass decision affects structural engineering, climate control design, and 15-year operational economics.

## PC vs Glass for Venlo-Style Greenhouses

| Factor | Glass (4mm tempered) | PC (16mm multiwall) |
|--------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Weight | 10 kg/m² | 2.8 kg/m² |
| U-Value | 5.7 W/m²K | 1.9 W/m²K |
| Light transmission | 90% | 78-82% |
| Structural steel | Heavy (supports glass weight) | Light (PC is 72% lighter) |
| Heating cost/yr (NL, 1ha) | €45,000-65,000 | €18,000-28,000 |
| Lifespan | 20-30 years | 10-15 years |

## The Dutch Contractor's Calculation

Glass greenhouses cost 15-25% more to build (structural steel for glass weight) but last longer. PC greenhouses cost less to build AND operate (heating savings) but require panel replacement at 10-15 years. The crossover point — where PC total cost of ownership beats glass — is approximately year 8-10 for Dutch climate conditions. For cold-climate exports (Canada, Scandinavia), PC crosses over at year 3-5 due to higher heating cost differential.

## Q: What do Dutch greenhouse builders actually specify?

For domestic Dutch projects: glass (tradition, lifespan, light transmission). For export projects to cold climates: PC multiwall (heating economics dominate). The trend is shifting toward PC even in the Netherlands as energy prices rise.
