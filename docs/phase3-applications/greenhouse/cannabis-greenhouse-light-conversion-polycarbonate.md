# Light-Conversion Polycarbonate Supply for Licensed Cannabis Producers

Licensed cannabis producers operate under strict regulations where every gram of yield is tracked and every input cost is scrutinized. Light-conversion PC directly impacts the two metrics that matter: grams per watt and grams per square foot.

## Cannabis Greenhouse Requirements

| Requirement | Spec | Standard |
|------------|------|----------|
| Light optimization | Maximum PAR per watt of supplemental lighting | Cannabis Regulations SOR/2018-144 |
| Security glazing | Impact resistant, forced-entry delay | Health Canada security directive |
| UV management | PAR conversion preferred (UV→red/blue) | GPP compliance |
| Opacity | Opaque option for photoperiod control | Light deprivation compliance |
| Traceability | Batch-level for GPP audit | Cannabis Act Section 62 |

## Light-Conversion Economics for Cannabis

Standard PC blocks UV — a wasted energy source. Light-conversion PC converts UV to PAR at 660nm (red) and 450nm (blue). In Canadian licensed production, trial data shows: 15-20% yield increase, 1-2 weeks faster flowering cycle, and 8-12% reduction in supplemental lighting hours. ROI: panel premium recovered within first harvest cycle.

## Regulatory Compliance

Health Canada's Good Production Practices (GPP) require: batch traceability of all materials contacting the product zone, documented material specifications, and change control for any material substitution. Light-conversion PC panels must be documented as a GPP-controlled material with certificate of analysis.
