# Anti-Condensation Polycarbonate Supply for Hydroponic Greenhouse Builders

Hydroponic greenhouses operate at 80-95% relative humidity. Condensation on the inner panel surface is the #1 operational problem — it drips onto plants (fungal disease), reduces light (10-15% loss), and accelerates algae in panel flutes.

## Anti-Condensation Technology Options

| Technology | Mechanism | Lifespan | Relative Cost |
|-----------|----------|----------|---------------|
| Co-extruded anti-fog | Hydrophilic layer in surface | 10-15yr (panel life) | $$ |
| Spray-on anti-fog | Periodic application | 1-2 seasons | $ |
| Anti-drip tape | Adhesive strip at panel bottom | 3-5 years | $ |
| Heated panels | Electrical heating element | 10yr+ | $$$ |

## Specifying Anti-Condensation for Hydroponics

For commercial hydroponic facilities: co-extruded anti-fog is the only permanent solution. The hydrophilic surface spreads condensation into a microscopically thin, transparent water film that flows down the panel (via gravity) rather than forming droplets that drip.

## Q: Does anti-fog coating affect light transmission?

A properly applied anti-fog coating actually increases effective light transmission by 8-12% in high-humidity conditions — not by changing the panel's optical properties, but by preventing the condensation droplets that scatter and reflect incoming light.
