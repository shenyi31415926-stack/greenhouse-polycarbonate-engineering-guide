# 16mm vs 25mm Multiwall Polycarbonate for Year-Round Cold-Climate Greenhouse Operations

The thickness decision is the most consequential specification a cold-climate greenhouse operator makes — it determines heating costs for 10-15 years.

## 16mm vs 25mm: The Numbers

| Factor | 16mm Triple-Wall | 25mm Multi-Wall |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| U-Value | 1.9 W/m²K | 1.5 W/m²K |
| PAR transmission | 78-80% | 72-75% |
| Weight | 3.0 kg/m² | 4.7 kg/m² |
| Snow load capacity | 1.5-2.0 kN/m² | 2.5-3.5 kN/m² |
| Cost/sqm (panel only) | $14-20 | $22-30 |
| Annual heating cost (1ha, Alberta) | $32,000-38,000 | $24,000-28,000 |
| Annual heating savings | — | $6,000-10,000 |

## The Breakeven Calculation

25mm costs ~$8-10/sqm more than 16mm. For a 10,000 sqm greenhouse: $80,000-100,000 additional upfront cost. At $8,000/year average heating savings: 10-12.5 year breakeven. Above 10,000 sqm, 25mm is the economically rational choice for zones 3-4. Below 5,000 sqm, 16mm with supplemental heating is usually more cost-effective.

## Light vs Heat: The Tradeoff

25mm loses 5-6% more PAR than 16mm due to additional internal walls. For high-light crops (tomatoes, peppers): 16mm with more supplemental lighting may outperform 25mm with less light. For low-light crops (leafy greens, cannabis vegetative): 25mm with its heating savings wins. Light-conversion PC changes this calculus — it recovers some of the lost PAR through UV conversion, reducing the light penalty of thicker panels.
