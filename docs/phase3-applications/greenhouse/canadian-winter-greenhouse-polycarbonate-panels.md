# Polycarbonate Greenhouse Panels for Canadian Winter Growing

Canadian commercial greenhouse operators face the most demanding PC panel requirements: -30°C exterior, +20°C interior, 50°C differential across 16mm of polycarbonate.

## Cold-Climate Panel Requirements

| Requirement | Spec | Why |
|------------|------|-----|
| U-Value | < 1.7 W/m²K | -30°C to +20°C = 50°C ΔT |
| Thickness | 16-25mm multiwall | Heat loss scales with ΔT |
| Snow load | 2.5+ kN/m² | Ontario/Quebec building code |
| Light transmission | > 78% PAR | Short winter daylight hours |
| UV management | Light-conversion preferred | Winter UV is peak season |

## Canadian Greenhouse Economics

Heating cost is 30-40% of operational expenses for Canadian greenhouses. Every 0.1 W/m²K improvement in U-value saves approximately $1,200/year in heating for a 1,000 sqm greenhouse at Ontario natural gas prices. Light-conversion panels add PAR without increasing heating load — the only technology addressing both sides of the winter growing equation simultaneously.
