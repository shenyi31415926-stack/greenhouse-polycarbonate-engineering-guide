# Polycarbonate Greenhouse Covering for Commercial Nursery Propagators

Nursery propagation has unique glazing requirements: young plants are photosensitive, humidity is maximum, and temperature stability is critical for germination success.

## Nursery-Grade PC Requirements

| Requirement | Spec | Why |
|------------|------|-----|
| Light diffusion | > 80% | Prevent seedling scorch from direct sun |
| UV management | Partial transmission or conversion | UV triggers beneficial stress responses |
| Anti-fog | Co-extruded | 95%+ RH during germination |
| Thermal stability | U-value < 2.2 | Germination temperature ±1°C tolerance |
| Thickness | 8-10mm | Balance of light vs insulation |

## Diffusion: The Key Specification

Seedlings and cuttings are photosensitive — direct sunlight scorches leaves and kills young plants before they develop UV tolerance. Diffusion panels scatter incoming light, creating uniform illumination without hot spots. Spec: diffusion > 80%, measured by ASTM D1003 haze meter in transmission mode.

## Q: What color/tint for propagation houses?

Opal (white) diffusion panels — not clear. Clear panels create light hot spots that kill seedlings at the bench edge while center plants are light-starved. Opal diffusion evens out the light distribution across the entire bench.
