# How to Verify Polycarbonate Factory Quality Remotely

Not every buyer can visit a factory in China, Germany, or Israel. Here's a systematic remote verification process that catches 90% of quality issues before you place an order.

## The Remote Audit Protocol

### Step 1: Request the Document Package

A serious factory will provide all of these within 48 hours:

- IATF 16949 or ISO 9001 certificate (with verifiable certificate number)
- Raw material purchase invoices (last 3 months, showing Covestro/SABIC resin)
- Batch record from the most recent export order (redacted client info OK)
- Video tour of the production line (not edited marketing footage — ask for today's date shown on screen)
- 3 client references in your region

**Red flag:** Takes more than 1 week to provide any of these.

### Step 2: Verify the Certificate

For IATF 16949: Check the certificate number on the IAF CertSearch database (free, public). Verify: factory name, address, and scope ("polycarbonate sheet extrusion" must appear). Check: issue date, expiry date, and certifying body accreditation. A common scam: showing an IATF 16949 certificate from an unaccredited body.

### Step 3: Order and Test Samples

Order 3-5 A4-sized samples from the factory. Pay for them — free samples are marketing, paid samples are procurement. Test:

| Test | Method | Pass Criteria |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Thickness consistency | Digital micrometer, 9 points across panel | ±0.1mm (IATF) or ±0.3mm (ISO) |
| UV coating presence | UV flashlight test — coated side should block/convert | Visible difference between sides |
| Light transmission | PAR meter or smartphone lux meter | Within 5% of spec sheet |
| Visual clarity | Backlight against printed text | Text readable without distortion |
| Haze | Visual against black background | No milky cloudiness |

### Step 4: Commission a Third-Party Inspection

Services: SGS, TÜV Rheinland, Bureau Veritas, AsiaInspection. Cost: $300-500 for a basic factory audit, $800-1,500 for a comprehensive production line audit.

Provide the inspector with a checklist: verify the extrusion line matches the video, check resin storage (Makrolon/Lexan drums labeled), review QC lab equipment, photograph the batch records for your order.

### Step 5: Run a Trial Order

Before committing to a 10,000 sqm order: order 200-500 sqm as a trial. Ship to your location. Install a test section. Monitor for 2-3 months (ideally through one season change). Test: light transmission before and after, thickness re-check, visual inspection for yellowing or delamination.

### Step 6: Verify Shipping Documentation

For the trial order, verify: bill of lading matches the factory address (not a trading company office), certificate of origin (Form A or FTA), and packing list with batch numbers matching your QC records.

## Remote Verification Scorecard

| Check | Weight | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Certificate verified online | 25% | ☐ |
| Sample testing passed | 25% | ☐ |
| Third-party audit | 20% | ☐ |
| Trial order | 20% | ☐ |
| Client reference confirmed | 10% | ☐ |

**Score > 80%:** Proceed with confidence. **50-79%:** Proceed with caution, additional verification needed. **< 50%:** Do not proceed.
