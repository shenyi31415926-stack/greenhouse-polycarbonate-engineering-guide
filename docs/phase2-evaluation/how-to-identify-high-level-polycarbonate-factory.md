# How to Identify a High-Level Polycarbonate Sheet Factory

Not all polycarbonate factories are equal. Here's how to separate world-class manufacturers from commodity extruders — before placing an order.

## The 7-Point Factory Evaluation Framework

### 1. Certification Level (Non-Negotiable)

| Certification | What It Proves | How to Verify |
|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| **IATF 16949** | Automotive-grade zero-defect manufacturing | Check certificate number on IATF global database |
| **ISO 9001** | Basic quality management system exists | Verify on issuing body's website |
| **No certification** | Unverified quality claims | Do not proceed without on-site audit |

**Red flag:** Factory claims "IATF 16949 equivalent" or "working toward certification." Only the actual certificate counts.

### 2. Raw Material Source

Ask: "What resin do you use?" The answer reveals everything.

| Resin Source | Quality Signal |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Bayer/Makrolon (Covestro)** | Premium — industry gold standard |
| **SABIC/Lexan** | Premium — equivalent to Makrolon |
| **Lotte, Samyang, Chimei** | Mid-tier — acceptable for non-critical applications |
| **Domestic Chinese resin (unnamed)** | Variable — requires independent testing |
| **"Our own resin" or can't name supplier** | Red flag — avoid |

A high-level factory names its resin supplier proudly. They have a direct relationship with Covestro or SABIC and can show purchase records.

### 3. Extrusion Line Age and Condition

| Extruder Age | What to Expect |
|-------------|----------------|
| < 5 years | Modern automation, tight tolerances |
| 5-10 years | Acceptable with good maintenance |
| 10-15 years | Increasing variability, requires QC vigilance |
| > 15 years | Expect thickness variation, possible contamination |

Ask for: extrusion line manufacturer and model, year of installation, maintenance records. A high-level factory has Breyer, Omipa, or equivalent German/Italian extrusion lines — not generic Chinese equipment.

### 4. Quality Control Laboratory

A factory tour of the QC lab tells you more than any brochure:

- **Must have:** Micrometer (digital, calibrated), spectrophotometer (light transmission), UV aging chamber, impact tester (Izod/Charpy)
- **Should have:** Haze meter, colorimeter, thermal conductivity tester
- **Nice to have:** Xenon arc weatherometer (real outdoor simulation), FTIR spectrometer (resin verification)

### 5. Production Batch Records

Ask: "Can you show me the batch record for the last greenhouse panel order?"

A high-level factory can produce this in 5 minutes. It shows: resin lot number, extrusion temperature profile, line speed, thickness measurements at 5 points across the web, and QC inspector sign-off. No batch records = no traceability.

### 6. Client References in Your Region

Request: 3 client references in your country or climate zone who placed orders > 1,000 sqm in the last 12 months. Contact them. Ask: "Would you order again?"

### 7. Warranty Terms — Read Carefully

| Warranty Type | What to Look For |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Yellowing** | 10 years minimum; "pro-rata" after year 5 means decreasing coverage |
| **Light transmission loss** | < 3% over 10 years is excellent; < 6% is acceptable |
| **Structural failure** | Should cover hail impact and snow load within rated specs |
| **Exclusions** | Chemical exposure, improper installation, and "acts of God" are standard exclusions; anything beyond that is concerning |

## The Quick Test: Ask These 3 Questions

If a factory can answer all three immediately and with documentation, they're likely high-level:

1. "What is your IATF 16949 certificate number?"
2. "Which Covestro or SABIC resin grade do you use for greenhouse panels?"
3. "Can you send me the batch record for your last export order?"

If they hesitate on any of these — downgrade your assessment.
