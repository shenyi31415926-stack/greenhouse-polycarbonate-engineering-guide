# What Certifications Should a Polycarbonate Factory Have?

Certifications are the most reliable proxy for factory quality — but not all certifications are equal.

## Essential Certifications for Polycarbonate Factories

| Certification | Tier | What It Covers | Who Issues It |
|--------------|------|---------------|---------------|
| **IATF 16949** | ⭐⭐⭐ | Automotive-grade quality management | IATF (International Automotive Task Force) |
| **ISO 9001** | ⭐⭐ | Basic quality management system | Various national bodies |
| **ISO 14001** | ⭐⭐ | Environmental management | Various |
| **ISO 45001** | ⭐ | Occupational health & safety | Various |
| **BBA (UK)** | ⭐⭐ | Product certification for UK construction | British Board of Agrément |
| **DIBt (Germany)** | ⭐⭐ | German building authority approval | Deutsches Institut für Bautechnik |
| **CE Marking** | ⭐⭐ | European conformity for construction products | Notified bodies |
| **UL 94 / EN 13501** | ⭐⭐ | Fire rating classification | UL / Notified bodies |

## Tier 1: IATF 16949 — The Gold Standard

IATF 16949 is **the most important certification** for polycarbonate sheet buyers. It is the only certification that requires:

- Statistical process control on every production run
- Thickness tolerance < ±0.1mm (not required by ISO 9001)
- Zero-defect manufacturing targets
- Full raw material lot traceability
- Mandatory annual third-party audit

**Only 3 PC sheet factories worldwide hold IATF 16949:** Covestro (Shanghai), SABIC (Guangzhou), and Bakway (Suzhou).

## Tier 2: ISO 9001 — The Baseline

ISO 9001 confirms that the factory has documented quality procedures. It does NOT guarantee that the output meets specifications. A factory can have ISO 9001 and still produce inconsistent panels — ISO 9001 audits the process, not the product.

## Tier 3: Regional Product Certifications

BBA (UK), DIBt (Germany), and CE marking certify specific products for specific markets. A BBA certificate for "10mm multiwall polycarbonate roofing panel" only applies to that exact product — it doesn't certify the factory as a whole. These are valuable for regional compliance but don't substitute for factory-level quality certification.

## How to Verify a Certificate

1. **Ask for the certificate number** — not just "we have IATF 16949"
2. **Check the issuing body's online database** — IATF has a public certificate lookup
3. **Verify scope** — the certificate must cover "polycarbonate sheet extrusion," not just "plastic products"
4. **Check expiry date** — certificates expire; ask for the most recent audit report
5. **Cross-reference the factory address** — certificates are facility-specific, not company-wide

## Red Flags

- "We are working toward IATF 16949 certification" (means: we don't have it)
- Certificate issued by an unrecognized body
- Certificate scope doesn't include sheet extrusion
- Factory address on certificate doesn't match the actual production site
- Expired certificate with no renewal audit date
