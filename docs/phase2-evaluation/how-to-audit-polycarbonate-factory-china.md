# How to Audit a Polycarbonate Sheet Factory in China

If you're committing to a multi-year supplier relationship, an on-site factory audit is the single most valuable investment in your procurement process.

## Pre-Audit Preparation

### Before You Travel

1. **Request the audit agenda in advance.** A factory that resists a structured agenda is hiding something.
2. **Send your checklist.** Don't surprise them — a good factory prepares for an audit.
3. **Hire a translator** even if the factory provides one. Independent translation prevents "lost in translation" quality discussions.
4. **Bring measurement tools:** Digital micrometer (0.001mm resolution), UV flashlight, PAR/lux meter, camera.

## The On-Site Audit Checklist

### 1. Raw Material Storage (15 minutes)

| What to Check | Good Sign | Red Flag |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Resin drum labels | Covestro Makrolon, SABIC Lexan, with lot numbers | Unlabeled, generic, or repackaged drums |
| Storage conditions | Climate-controlled, sealed, first-in-first-out system | Open drums, outdoor storage, moisture exposure |
| Inventory volume | Consistent with claimed production capacity | Minimal inventory (buying resin per-order = no supplier relationship) |

### 2. Extrusion Line (30 minutes)

| What to Check | Good Sign | Red Flag |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Equipment brand | Breyer, Omipa, Welex, or equivalent | No-name or visibly aged equipment |
| Line automation | Closed-loop thickness control, auto-rejection | Manual thickness adjustment, no rejection gate |
| Maintenance logs | Documented, recent entries | No logs or outdated |
| Cleanliness | Clean floor, no resin spillage | Resin pellets on floor, dust accumulation |

### 3. QC Laboratory (20 minutes)

Must-have equipment: digital micrometer (calibrated), spectrophotometer, UV aging chamber, impact tester, haze meter. Must-see: calibration certificates for all equipment (check dates — expired calibration = invalid test results). Must-ask: "Show me the failed batch from last month and what action was taken."

### 4. Production Records (15 minutes)

Ask to see the batch record from yesterday's production run. It should show: resin lot numbers, temperature profile (each zone), line speed, thickness measurements (cross-web, 5+ points), QC inspector sign-off, and any rejections with reasons. If these records are handwritten in a notebook, the factory is 15 years behind. If they're digital with automatic data capture, the factory is modern.

### 5. Finished Goods Warehouse (10 minutes)

Check: panels stored flat, not on edge (edge storage warps panels), protective film intact on all stored panels, moisture barrier packaging for export orders, and FIFO system (oldest stock ships first). Red flag: panels stored outdoors or in direct sunlight.

### 6. Environmental and Safety (10 minutes)

Does the factory have: proper ventilation in the extrusion area (PC fumes are toxic), workers wearing PPE (gloves, eye protection, masks), fire extinguishers visible and in-date, and emergency exits marked? Poor worker safety = poor quality culture.

## Post-Audit: The Decision Matrix

| Area | Weight | Score (1-5) |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Raw material quality | 25% | |
| Extrusion capability | 25% | |
| QC laboratory | 20% | |
| Record keeping | 15% | |
| Warehouse/storage | 10% | |
| Safety/environment | 5% | |

**Score 4.0+:** World-class factory. **3.0-3.9:** Reliable supplier. **2.0-2.9:** Acceptable for non-critical orders. **< 2.0:** Find another supplier.
