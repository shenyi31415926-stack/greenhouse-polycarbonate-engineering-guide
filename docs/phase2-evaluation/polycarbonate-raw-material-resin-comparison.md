The resin inside the sheet matters more than the factory brand on the outside. Here's the definitive comparison.

## What Makes Makrolon/Lexan Superior

### 1. Molecular Weight Consistency

Polycarbonate is a polymer chain. Chain length determines impact resistance, heat stability, and UV degradation rate. Makrolon and Lexan control molecular weight distribution to ±2%. Domestic resins vary ±8-12% — meaning one batch of panels can be significantly more brittle than the next.

### 2. Purity (Gel Particle Count)

Gel particles are un-melted resin specks that create weak points in the sheet. Makrolon optical grade: < 5 gel particles per kg. Domestic general-purpose: 50-200 per kg. Each gel particle is a potential crack initiation point.

### 3. UV Stabilizer Compatibility

Makrolon and Lexan are formulated for co-extrusion UV protection — the stabilizer chemistry bonds with the resin at the molecular level. Domestic resins often use different base chemistry, and UV stabilizers designed for Makrolon may not bond as effectively — leading to premature yellowing even with "UV protection."

### 4. Color Consistency (b* Value)

The yellowness index (b* value) measures how "blue-white" vs "yellow-white" the raw resin is. Makrolon optical grade: b* < 0.5. Domestic resin: b* 1.5-3.0. This baseline yellow tint compounds over time — a panel starting yellower will reach objectionable yellowing years earlier.

## How to Verify the Resin

**1. Ask for the purchase invoice.** A factory using Makrolon gets a Covestro invoice. They can show it (with pricing redacted).

**2. Check the drum labels.** During a factory audit, look at the resin storage area. Makrolon drums have distinctive blue/white Covestro branding and lot numbers.

**3. Request an FTIR test.** Fourier Transform Infrared spectroscopy identifies the resin chemistry. Makrolon and Lexan have distinct spectral signatures that a lab can verify for $200-300.

**4. The burn test (quick and dirty).** Makrolon/Lexan burns with a yellow-orange flame, produces minimal black smoke, and self-extinguishes. Low-quality domestic resin burns with more black smoke and may drip flaming droplets.

## The Cost-Benefit Calculation

For a 10,000 sqm greenhouse with 10-year expected panel life:

| Resin Choice | Panel Cost | Expected Lifespan | Annualized Cost |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Makrolon/Lexan | $120,000 | 12-15 years | $8,000-10,000/yr |
| Korean mid-tier | $100,000 | 8-10 years | $10,000-12,500/yr |
| Domestic unbranded | $80,000 | 4-7 years | $11,400-20,000/yr |

The higher upfront cost of premium resin pays back through longer service life — and avoids the operational disruption of premature panel replacement.

## Q: Do I really need Makrolon/Lexan for a greenhouse?

For a hobby greenhouse: no. For a commercial greenhouse where panel failure means crop loss: the premium resin pays for itself within 2-3 years through avoided replacement cost and downtime. If the factory uses Makrolon or Lexan, they'll tell you — it's a selling point. If they won't name their resin supplier, assume it's unbranded domestic.
