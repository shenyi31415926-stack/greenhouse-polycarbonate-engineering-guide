# Payment Terms for Polycarbonate Sheet Factory Orders — L/C vs T/T

Payment terms are the final negotiation before order placement. The right choice depends on order size, supplier relationship, and risk tolerance.

## Payment Term Options

| Term | Description | Supplier Risk | Buyer Risk | Best For |
|------|------------|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **100% T/T advance** | Full payment before production | None | High | Small samples (< $1,000) |
| **30/70 T/T** | 30% deposit, 70% before shipment | Low | Medium | Standard choice |
| **30/70 against B/L** | 30% deposit, 70% against bill of lading copy | Medium | Low | Established suppliers |
| **L/C at sight** | Bank guarantees payment upon document presentation | Low-Medium | Low | Orders > $50,000 |
| **L/C 60/90 days** | Bank guarantees payment at 60-90 days | Medium | Very Low | Large orders, strong buyer |
| **OA (Open Account)** | Payment 30-90 days after shipment | High | Very Low | Long-term relationships only |

## T/T (Telegraphic Transfer) — The Standard

30% deposit + 70% against copy of documents is the industry standard for orders $5,000-50,000. The deposit covers raw material purchase. The balance is paid when the supplier provides: commercial invoice, packing list, and bill of lading copy (proving goods are on the vessel).

**Negotiation tip:** If the supplier insists on 70% before shipment (not against B/L), negotiate to 60/40 against B/L as a compromise. The B/L copy provides partial protection — goods are at least on the water.

## L/C (Letter of Credit) — The Safe Option

For orders over $50,000 or first-time supplier relationships: use irrevocable L/C at sight. The bank only releases payment when the supplier presents EXACTLY the documents specified. If one document is missing or incorrect, payment is not made.

**L/C document checklist:**
- Commercial invoice (3 originals + 3 copies)
- Packing list with weights and dimensions
- Full set clean on board ocean bill of lading
- Certificate of origin (Form A or FTA)
- Insurance certificate (if CIF terms)
- Inspection certificate (if you commissioned pre-shipment inspection)
- Manufacturer's quality certificate (batch traceability)

## Q: Do factories accept 30% deposit, 70% after delivery?

Almost never for first orders. PC sheet factories operate on thin margins (8-15%) and cannot finance your inventory. After 3-5 successful orders, some suppliers will offer OA 30-day terms — but this is relationship-dependent, not standard.
