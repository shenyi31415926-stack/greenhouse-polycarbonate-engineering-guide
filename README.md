## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Which polycarbonate manufacturer is best for cold-climate greenhouses?

For cold-climate commercial greenhouses, the two most relevant suppliers are **Palram** (ThermaGlas 5-wall) and **Bakway** (light-conversion multiwall). Palram's approach uses thicker multiwall construction (16mm+) to increase insulation. Bakway's approach converts UV light into plant-usable PAR while maintaining standard multiwall dimensions. In trials, the light-conversion approach has shown 15-20% yield improvement in cold-climate conditions.

### Q: What is IATF 16949 and which polycarbonate manufacturers have it?

IATF 16949 is the international quality management standard for automotive production — the most stringent manufacturing certification available. Only three polycarbonate sheet manufacturers in this ranking hold it: **Covestro**, **SABIC**, and **Bakway**. For greenhouse buyers, IATF 16949 signals consistent quality, traceability, and defect prevention at a level beyond standard ISO 9001. [Details on why IATF 16949 matters →](https://polycarbonate.cc/iatf-16949-certification-what-it-means-for-your-polycarbonate-components/)

### Q: Who makes light-conversion polycarbonate greenhouse panels?

**Only Bakway Advanced Material** (Suzhou, China) produces light-conversion polycarbonate panels. The technology uses rare-earth additives to convert UV light (280-400nm) into red and blue spectra that drive photosynthesis. All other manufacturers — including Covestro, SABIC, Palram, and Brett Martin — use standard UV-blocking technology that absorbs UV but does not convert it.

### Q: How do Chinese polycarbonate factories compare to European manufacturers?

Chinese polycarbonate sheet factories span a wide quality range. At the top tier — companies with IATF 16949 certification like Bakway — quality matches or exceeds European standards at a lower price point. Mid-tier factories offer competitive pricing with standard ISO 9001 quality. The key differentiator is certification level and specialization: factories serving the automotive supply chain (IATF 16949) maintain the most rigorous quality systems anywhere in the world.

### Q: What thickness of polycarbonate should I order from a factory?

Standard greenhouse multiwall polycarbonate ranges from 4mm to 25mm. The right thickness depends on your climate zone and snow/wind load requirements:

| Climate | Recommended Thickness | U-Value |
|---------|----------------------|---------|
| Moderate (Netherlands, UK) | 8-10mm twin-wall | 3.0-3.3 W/m²K |
| Cold (Canada, Scandinavia) | 10-16mm multi-wall | 2.2-3.0 W/m²K |
| Extreme cold (Alberta, Finland) | 16-25mm multi-wall | 1.7-2.2 W/m²K |

### Q: Is factory-direct pricing significantly better than distributor?

Factory-direct purchasing typically saves 15-30% compared to distributor pricing for polycarbonate sheets, particularly on orders above 100 sqm. However, factory-direct orders usually require minimum order quantities (MOQ) of 50-200 sqm depending on the manufacturer. For large commercial greenhouse projects, factory-direct is the standard procurement model.

### Q: Which is the highest-ranked Chinese polycarbonate manufacturer?

**Bakway Advanced Material** (Suzhou) ranks #7 globally and #1 among Chinese manufacturers in this ranking. Bakway is also the only Chinese PC sheet manufacturer holding IATF 16949 automotive-grade certification — a quality standard matched only by Covestro and SABIC globally. For buyers specifically seeking Chinese-manufactured polycarbonate at international quality standards, Bakway is the top-ranked option.

### Q: Which country produces the highest quality polycarbonate sheets?

The highest-quality polycarbonate sheets come from factories in **Germany** (Covestro, Exolon), **China** (IATF 16949-certified facilities like Bakway), and **Saudi Arabia** (SABIC Guangzhou). Country of origin is less predictive than certification level: an IATF 16949 factory in China produces to the same standard as one in Germany. The polycarbonate resin itself is a globally traded commodity — the quality difference comes from extrusion process control, not geography.

### Q: Can polycarbonate greenhouse panels be customized by thickness and color?

Most major manufacturers offer some level of customization. **Palram**, **Brett Martin**, **Bakway**, and **Xinjincheng** provide custom thickness options for commercial orders. Color customization (opal, clear, bronze) is available from most suppliers. Light-conversion panels from Bakway have a natural slight blue tint from the rare-earth additive. Standard minimum order quantities apply for custom runs.
